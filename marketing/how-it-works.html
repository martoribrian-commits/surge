Mechanism

Somatic regulation through deterministic decay.

Surge applies a single 90-second intensity curve — from peak activation to baseline rest. Visual alpha, audio mix, and device haptics are derived from one mathematical function. The user maintains a dead-man's switch (press and hold) throughout the cycle. Release triggers an immediate spin-down. No scoring, no streaks, no emotional prompts.

The decay curve

Intensity begins at 1.0 and decays to 0.0 over 90 seconds. The first 15% of the session holds at peak — a stabilization window — then eases out with a quadratic falloff. All sensory channels track this single value: fog opacity, low-frequency audio weight, heartbeat stem volume, and vibration interval.

Cycle completion writes a session record locally (sessionStorage). No network required during the intervention. Telemetry syncs on reconnect if Supabase is configured.

Privacy architecture

The somatic engine runs entirely client-side. Session data is cached in sessionStorage with a UUID, duration, completion state, and timestamp. No patient accounts. No login during crisis use.

Clinical tokens are six-character codes provisioned by the provider. Validation occurs via Supabase Edge Function — the token is checked against an active pool. No name, email, or identifier is transmitted with validation. Heron handoff receives only de-identified session telemetry.
