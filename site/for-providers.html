The clinical gap

Patients arrive dysregulated, or dysregulate between sessions. Your clinical time is too valuable to spend on baseline recovery. Surge gives patients a deterministic somatic tool they can use in the parking lot, the bathroom, or at 2 a.m., without creating another account or another relationship to manage.

How it works for your practice

You provision Clinical Tokens through the Surge provider portal. Each token is a six-character code with a set expiry and use count. Patients enter the token once. No login. No password. No PHI transmitted at the point of access.

You see activation counts and session completion signals. You do not see session content unless the patient brings it into your room.

What your patients experience

Ninety seconds. Press and hold. Visual and haptic intensity decay on a fixed curve. When the cycle completes, the screen softens and holds. Optionally, they can enter Crane, a recovery guide that offers presence without therapy-speak or diagnosis.

If they lock their phone mid-cycle, the system pauses. They hold to resume. No data loss. No penalty. No streak broken.

Privacy architecture

The somatic engine runs entirely in the browser. Session records write to sessionStorage on the device. Token validation checks a hashed lookup server-side and returns only valid or invalid. No patient name. No provider name. No session transcript leaves the device unless the patient initiates a Crane conversation, which is not persisted between visits.
